Privacy notice

This privacy notice explains how we collect and use your personal information for the service design maturity survey.

Who we are

The service design maturity survey is operated by the Department for Education (DfE).

For the purpose of data protection legislation, DfE is the data controller for the personal data processed as part of this service.

How we will use your information

We collect and process the following information when you use this service:

  • your department code
  • your organisation name
  • your responses to the survey questions
  • information about how you use the service (if you accept analytics cookies)

We use this information to:

  • provide the service to you
  • measure and improve the service
  • understand how organisations are developing their service design capability

Legal basis for processing your data

Our legal basis for processing your personal data is:

  • Article 6(1)(e) of the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) – the processing is necessary for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest
  • Article 6(1)(f) of the UK GDPR – the processing is necessary for our legitimate interests in understanding and improving service design capability across government

How long we keep your data

We will only retain your personal data for as long as:

  • it is needed for the purposes set out in this document
  • the law requires us to

Your rights

Under the UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • access your personal data
  • have your personal data rectified if it is inaccurate or incomplete
  • request the deletion or removal of personal data where there is no compelling reason for its continued processing
  • restrict our processing of your personal data (i.e. permitting its storage but no further processing)
  • object to direct marketing (including profiling) and processing for statistical purposes
  • not be subject to decisions based purely on automated processing where it produces a legal or similarly significant effect on you
